Jewellery Buying Guide: Common Questions Answered

Q: What's the difference between 22K and 24K gold?

Answer: 22K gold is 91.67% pure with 8.33% alloy metals, making it harder and more suitable for Jewellery. 24K gold is 99.99% pure but too soft for daily wear. In India, 22K is the standard for Jewellery; 24K is for investment bars and coins.

Q: Is hallmarked Jewellery more expensive?

Answer: Hallmarked Jewellery costs slightly more (hallmarking fee ₹50-100 per piece) but guarantees purity and provides resale value. It's legally required in India for Jewellery above 20 grams, ensuring consumer protection.

Q: Can I sell my Jewellery back to SGV Jewellers?

Answer: Yes. We buy back hallmarked 22K Jewellery at rates based on current gold prices and Jewellery weight. Bring your purchase bill and Jewellery; we'll appraise it within 10 minutes and make an offer. Payment available in cash or adjusted toward new purchases.

Q: How much gold does a bride need for a wedding?

Answer: An Indian bride typically wears 100-300 grams of gold Jewellery, depending on region and customs. North Indian brides average 150-200g; South Indian brides wear 200-300g. This includes necklaces, bangles, earrings, rings, and nose pieces.

How Gold Jewellery is Priced: Complete Breakdown

Gold Jewellery price = (Gold Weight in grams × Current Gold Rate) + Making Charges

Example Calculation:

  • Gold weight: 10 grams
  • Current gold rate: ₹6,200 per gram
  • Gold value: 10g × ₹6,200 = ₹62,000
  • Making charges (for simple design): ₹6,200 (10% of gold value)
  • Hallmarking: ₹45 per piece (mandatory in India)
  • GST: 3% of gold value
